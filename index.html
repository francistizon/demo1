The Asian water monitor (Varanus salvator) is a large varanid lizard native to South and Southeast Asia. It is widely considered to be the second-largest lizard species, after the Komodo dragon. It is distributed from eastern and northeastern India and Bangladesh, the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Sri Lanka, through southern China and Hainan Island in the east to mainland Southeast Asia and the islands of Sumatra, Borneo, Java, Lombok, the Riau Archipelago, and Sulawesi. The Asian water monitor is a large, heavy-bodied lizard with a long tail and strong limbs. The Asian water monitor is known for its intelligence and adaptability, making it a popular choice among reptile enthusiasts. However, they require a spacious and secure enclosure, as well as a varied diet to thrive in captivity. The Asian water monitor is dark brown or blackish with yellow spots on the underside that fade gradually with age. It has powerful jaws, serrated teeth and sharp claws.
